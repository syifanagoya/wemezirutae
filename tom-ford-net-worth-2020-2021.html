Early Life

He briefly studied at the Bard College at Simon’s Rock and dropped out, and later Tom Ford went to New York in an attempt to finish studying and graduating with art and history degree from the New York University, but he dropped out to go on with commercials acting career.

Career

Before graduating, Tom Ford was in Paris for an internship at the Chloé luxury fashion house. After he received his degree, he kept trying to get to Cathy Hardwick fashion designer to get a job, it did work, and he worked for Cathy as an assistant for two years, mainly a design assistant.

Tom Ford worked for Perry Ellis after his first work with Cathy Hardwick, but he also left after two years stating that if he wants to be a successful designer he needs to leave America, because of the fashion trends there. So, he landed a job at the Italian luxury brand “Gucci,” and after a year he took the design director position. He contributed a lot to the company that they had to bring four people after he left in 2004 to do the work he was doing. Tom Ford launched his self-titled label after leaving Gucci.

For the TV work, Tom Ford is the director, producer, and writer for two movies. The first movie is a romantic drama titled “A Single Man,” and it was released in 2009. The second one is a psychological thriller drama movie released in 2016 titled “Nocturnal Animals.”

Awards & Achievements

Tom Ford received many award winnings and nominations for his debut movie as a director “A Single Man.” He won the Bodil Award for Best American Film, Queer Lion trophy, German Art House Cinemas Guild Film Award – Gold, CinEuphoria Award for Top Ten of the Year – Audience Award, and CinEuphoria Award for Top Ten of the Year – International Competition.

Furthermore, Tom Ford received more awards for his most recent and 2nd movie “Nocturnal Animals.” He won the 2017 David di Donatello Miglior Film Straniero, Grand Jury Prize, Hollywood Breakthrough Directing Award, and the Sierra Award for Best Screenplay, Adapted.
