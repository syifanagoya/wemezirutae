FAQs about Nikolai Demidenko

Who is Nikolai Demidenko?

Nikolai Demidenko is a celebrated Russian-born concert pianist known for his technical brilliance and musical vision, making him a leading exponent of the Russian school of piano playing.

What is he famous for?

He is renowned for his performances of the Russian concerto repertoire, including works by Prokofiev, Rachmaninov, and Tchaikovsky, and has collaborated with numerous orchestras and conductors worldwide.

Has Nikolai Demidenko won any awards?

Yes, he won a Gramophone Award in 1992 in the concerto category for his recording of the Medtner Piano Concertos No. 2 and 3.

What kind of music does Demidenko specialize in?

Besides the standard Germanic and Russian repertory, Demidenko specializes in the works of Chopin and champions neglected composers like Muzio Clementi, Carl Maria von Weber, and others, as well as transcriptions by Ferruccio Busoni.

How extensive is Demidenko’s discography?

His discography consists of nearly 40 CDs, including over 20 albums for Hyperion Records and recordings for other labels such as AGPL and Onyx Classics.

Did he receive any academic recognition?

Yes, he was awarded an honorary doctorate from the University of Surrey in 2014 for his outstanding contribution to music and the university.

Where was Nikolai Demidenko born?

He was born on July 1, 1955, in Anisimovo, Russia.

What educational background does Demidenko have?

Demidenko studied at the Gnessin State Musical College and the Moscow Conservatoire under Dmitri Bashkirov.

Has Demidenko performed in the United States?

Yes, he has performed in the United States, including a recital at the 15th International Keyboard Institute and Festival at Mannes College in New York City in 2013.

What are some notable performances or recordings by Demidenko?

Notable recordings include his Bach-Busoni transcriptions on a Fazioli grand piano and his performance of Das Wohltemperierte Klavier for the 250th anniversary of J.S. Bach’s death, released on DVD.
